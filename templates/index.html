What is a Bell State?

A Bell State is the simplest form of quantum entanglement. We take two qubits that start at 0. We put the first qubit into a 50/50 superposition using a Hadamard (H) gate. Then, we connect them using a CNOT gate. This entangles them, making their outcomes permanently linked!

How does measurement affect entanglement?

Entanglement gives correlated results ONLY if both qubits are measured in matching directions. If Alice and Bob both measure Up/Down (Z) or both Left/Right (X), they get perfectly correlated matching results. If they measure in different bases, the quantum correlation is lost, and the results become completely random.

How does teleportation work?

We don't physically move a particle. Instead, Alice transfers the exact state of Qubit 0 to Bob's Qubit 2 by using an entangled helper pair (Q1 and Q2). Once Alice measures Q0 and Q1, she sends the result to Bob classically. Bob applies correction gates (CNOT and CZ) to reconstruct Alice's original state on his qubit!
